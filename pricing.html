Pricing (₦)

Pricing is based on complexity, scope, and turnaround. You are paying for engineered prompts (instruction systems), not written submissions.

Fair by scope

Single sections cost less than full dissertation systems. Multi-chapter systems are priced as reusable infrastructure.

Fair by level

Higher levels require more rigorous instruction architecture (depth control, synthesis control, and tighter constraints).

Fair by speed

Urgent builds cost more because they compress design + testing time.

How pricing is decided

Transparent logic. No “mystery pricing”.

Base scope rate

Section <→ Chapter <→ Packs <→ Full systems. Full systems include chapter-by-chapter instruction architecture and gating.

Complexity multiplier

Higher levels and complex rubrics require stronger structure control, evaluation constraints, and more testing.

Add-ons

Rubric alignment, citation enforcement, and workflow packs add value by improving execution accuracy and repeatability.

For institutional use: pricing depends on cohort size, consistency requirements, and governance constraints.

Why this is cheaper than “paying for the work”

Outsourced writing is a recurring expense and increases integrity risk. Engineered prompts are reusable infrastructure: you can apply the system repeatedly, adapt it, and improve it.
